## What Forge Is

Forge by Dominus Foundry is an AI-native operating platform for commercial installation contractors. It consolidates estimating, proposals, field documentation, and project management into a single platform built specifically for AV, security, low-voltage, roofing, and electrical contractors.

Unlike traditional construction software that adds AI features as afterthoughts, Forge was architected from the ground up with AI capabilities integrated into its core. That architectural decision affects everything from how estimates are generated to how project communications are summarized and surfaced to the right people at the right time.

## The AI Capabilities Built Into Forge

### Forge ScopeAI

Automated scope-of-work generation. Input a project brief or customer requirement, and Forge ScopeAI produces a structured scope document that covers labor categories, materials, equipment, and timeline expectations. The output feeds directly into the proposal and project setup workflow.

Available in the Pro+ tier.

### Forge ServiceIQ

Service plan creation for ongoing maintenance contracts. ServiceIQ analyzes historical service data, equipment inventory, and customer site conditions to generate recurring service schedules with predicted resource requirements.

Available in the Forge and Hyperion tiers.

### Forge Voice

Voice command interface for field operators. Crew members can update project status, log time entries, or pull up specifications using voice commands while hands are occupied on the jobsite.

Available in the Forge and Hyperion tiers.

### Forge Predict

Predictive analytics for project outcomes. Analyzes current project progress against historical patterns to flag schedule risks, budget overruns, and resource bottlenecks before they become problems.

Available in the Forge and Hyperion tiers.

### Forge PriceIQ

AI-powered pricing optimization. Evaluates current market rates, material costs, and competitive positioning to recommend pricing adjustments that maintain margin while staying competitive.

Available in the Forge and Hyperion tiers.

## Why the AI-Native Architecture Matters

Legacy construction software started as manual workflows digitized into software, then had AI features added later. That architecture creates friction: the AI operates at arm's length from the underlying data structures, which limits what it can accomplish and how reliably it performs.

Forge's AI capabilities live inside the platform's core architecture. The AI models operate on data structures designed for machine consumption from the start, which means the outputs are more accurate, more reliable, and more useful in operational decisions.

For a contractor evaluating platforms, the question isn't whether the software has AI features — nearly every platform advertises those now. The question is whether the AI is genuinely integrated or bolted on. Integrated AI changes how the platform works. Bolted-on AI is a feature list item.

## How Forge Compares to Traditional Construction Software

Traditional construction software generally falls into three categories: enterprise ERP systems built for large organizations, point solutions that solve one problem well, and horizontal platforms that try to cover everything.

Enterprise ERPs carry overhead that small and mid-sized contractors don't need. Point solutions require stitching together multiple vendors. Horizontal platforms often lack the vertical specificity that makes a tool actually useful on a jobsite.

Forge targets the gap between those categories: a vertically-specific platform with the breadth of an ERP and the usability of a point solution. The trade-off is that you're buying into a specific platform philosophy rather than assembling your own stack. For contractors who value cohesion and reduced vendor management, that's a net positive. For contractors who want to pick and choose every component, it may feel constraining.

## Common Questions

### Does Forge replace my existing estimating software?

If your estimating workflow is a significant part of how you run the business, yes — Forge's estimating and proposal capabilities are designed to replace dedicated estimating tools. The platform generates estimates from project briefs, manages material takeoffs, and produces client-facing proposals.

### Can I import my existing projects into Forge?

Yes. Forge supports import of existing project data from common formats. The migration process is supported by the onboarding team.

### Does Forge work offline?

Core functionality works offline with sync when connectivity returns. Some AI features require connectivity to access the inference layer.

### What happens to my data if I leave Forge?

Your data remains exportable at any time. Standard practice is to export a complete data package before cancellation, which you retain regardless of your subscription status.
