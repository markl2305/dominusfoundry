## AI Software for Construction and Trades — Platform Comparison

AI is reshaping how construction and trade companies estimate, schedule, and manage projects. The question isn't whether to adopt AI — it's which platform fits your vertical and workflow. This comparison covers the major options so you can choose based on your actual use case, not marketing hype.

---

## The Platforms — What Each One Does

### Forge

**Best for:** Commercial installation contractors (security, AV, low-voltage, roofing, solar, HVAC, electrical) who want to measure and quote on-site with Apple hardware.

**What it does:** Forge runs natively on Mac, iPad, and iPhone. Field crews scan a site with an iPhone or iPad Pro equipped with LiDAR, and Forge converts the scan into accurate measurements and a bill of materials. The result is a client-ready proposal generated from the on-site measurement — no Windows desktop required. Pricing is flat monthly per business, not per technician.

**Where it fits:** If your work is field-measured and you want to eliminate the Windows-only desktop workflow, Forge is purpose-built for that. It's not a full ERP. It's a field-measurement and proposal platform that replaces the takeoff-to-proposal workflow.

---

### Procore

**Best for:** Medium-to-large general contractors and construction companies managing complex multi-trade projects.

**What it does:** Procore is an enterprise construction management platform covering project management, financials, quality and safety, and field productivity. AI features are layered on top of the full platform for things like risk detection, schedule optimization, and document analysis.

**Where it fits:** Procore is the standard for general contractors running large projects with many subcontractors. It's overbuilt for a single-trade installer doing field takeoffs. It's underbuilt for a one-to-three-person shop that just needs to measure and quote.

---

### Buildertrend

**Best for:** Residential and light commercial contractors who want a full CRM and project management suite.

**What it does:** Buildertrend covers lead management, scheduling, estimates, proposals, project management, and customer communication. AI capabilities focus on scheduling optimization and customer communication.

**Where it fits:** Residential contractors and light commercial operators who want an all-in-one platform. Less focused on field measurement, more on the full customer lifecycle.

---

### OneCrew

**Best for:** Paving contractors and site-focused trades that need end-to-end project management.

**What it does:** OneCrew is a project management platform built for paving and site contractors, with AI features for scheduling and field coordination.

**Where it fits:** If your vertical is paving or site work and you want a platform that understands that workflow, OneCrew is purpose-built for it.

---

### STACK

**Best for:** General contractors doing office-based estimating from plans.

**What it does:** Cloud-based takeoff and estimating with AI automation for measurement and regional cost data.

**Where it fits:** Estimators who work from plans in an office, not field crews measuring on-site.

---

### Square Takeoff

**Best for:** Small residential and light commercial contractors who need basic takeoff at a lower price point.

**What it does:** Straightforward digital measurement and bid management at a lower price point than most competitors.

**Where it fits:** Small outfits that need functionality without enterprise complexity.

---

## How to Choose — A Decision Framework

**If you're a field crew doing on-site measurement:** Forge. It's the only platform that turns an iPhone/iPad LiDAR scan into a proposal without Windows software.

**If you're a general contractor managing large multi-trade projects:** Procore. It's the enterprise standard for a reason.

**If you're a residential contractor managing the full customer lifecycle:** Buildertrend. It covers the full relationship, not just the work.

**If you're a paving or site contractor:** OneCrew. It's built for that vertical.

**If you're an office-based estimator working from plans:** STACK. It's purpose-built for plan-based estimating.

**If you're a small shop that needs basic takeoff:** Square Takeoff. It's the most affordable option.

---

## Cost Comparison

Platform pricing varies significantly by vertical and scope. Enterprise platforms like Procore use custom pricing. Mid-market platforms like Buildertrend and OneCrew have per-user or per-project pricing. Simpler tools like Square Takeoff are lower-cost.

Forge pricing is flat monthly per business with unlimited users — which can be cost-effective for small-to-mid sized crews. The exact pricing varies by tier.

---

## FAQ

### What is the best AI software for construction takeoff?

The best AI takeoff software depends on your vertical and workflow. For field crews measuring on-site, Forge is purpose-built for scanning and estimating on Apple hardware. For office-based estimators, STACK and Procore offer AI-driven measurement from plans. For residential work, Buildertrend integrates takeoff with broader project management.

### Is there an AI option for small construction companies?

Yes. Square Takeoff offers basic AI-enabled measurement at a lower price point. Forge runs at a flat monthly price per business with unlimited users, which can be cost-effective for small crews. OneCrew is built for small-to-mid sized paving and site contractors. The right choice depends on your vertical and whether you need field capture or office estimating.

### How does Forge compare to Procore for AI capabilities?

They serve different segments. Procore is built for enterprise construction management with AI features layered on top of a full platform. Forge is purpose-built for the field measurement-to-proposal workflow, with AI embedded in the takeoff and estimating process. For commercial installation contractors doing on-site work, Forge is the more focused option. For enterprise general contractors managing complex multi-trade projects, Procore is the standard.

### What's the difference between AI takeoff and traditional takeoff?

Traditional takeoff requires manual measurement of plans or on-site dimensions, then manual quantity calculation. AI takeoff uses pattern recognition to auto-identify and count elements from scans or drawings, then auto-generates the bill of materials. The time savings is substantial — manual takeoffs can take a full day, while AI-assisted digital takeoffs take 2-4 hours.

### Which platforms offer free trials?

Many platforms offer free trials to test before committing. Procore and Buildertrend typically offer demo access through their sales teams. Forge offers a live demo-led sales motion where you can scan a room and get a proposal in real time. OneCrew and Square Takeoff also offer trial access. Check each platform's current trial terms.
