## TL;DR

Vertical SaaS for the trades is software built specifically for commercial installation contractors — AV, security, low-voltage, roofing, solar — with native support for their workflows. AI vertical SaaS adds machine learning to automate takeoff, proposals, documentation, and scheduling. For a five-person shop, the premium over horizontal tools pays for itself in reduced training time and eliminated workarounds.

## What is vertical SaaS?

Vertical SaaS is software purpose-built for a specific industry. Unlike horizontal platforms that serve many industries with generic features, vertical SaaS ships with industry-native workflows, terminology, and compliance baked in.

The distinction matters for commercial installation contractors. A horizontal CRM treats every customer the same. A vertical platform for AV integrators understands the difference between a control system, a display, and an audio component. A vertical platform for security contractors understands UL-listed equipment and AHJ requirements.

### How vertical differs from horizontal

| Dimension | Horizontal SaaS | Vertical SaaS |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| Target market | Any business | Specific industry |
| Out-of-box fit | Requires customization | Ready to use |
| Industry terminology | Generic | Native |
| Compliance handling | Manual | Built-in |
| Training time | Weeks to months | Days to weeks |

## Why vertical SaaS for the trades?

Commercial installation work involves complex, multi-step workflows that general-purpose tools don't understand. Takeoff, proposal, BOM, project management, crew dispatch, commissioning, closeout — each step has its own requirements, documents, and stakeholders.

A horizontal platform requires the contractor to build the workflow manually. A vertical platform ships with the workflow. The difference shows up in adoption: a team that would need six months to configure a horizontal platform often goes live on vertical SaaS in two weeks.

### The AI layer

AI vertical SaaS adds machine learning to those native workflows. Automatic takeoff from LiDAR scans. AI-generated proposals from scan data. Automated documentation from recorded calls. Predictive scheduling based on historical patterns.

The AI doesn't replace judgment — it removes the manual work that sits between judgment and execution. An estimator still decides what to specify. The AI generates the proposal that communicates the specification to the client.

## Platform landscape by trade

Different trades have different dominant platforms. The following table maps major vertical SaaS offerings to the trades they serve.

| Trade | Leading Platforms | Key Differentiator |
|-------|------------------|--------------------|
| AV integration | D-Tools, Forge | D-Tools owns the legacy market; Forge is the AI-native challenger |
| Security/Low-voltage | D-Tools, Forge | Same as AV — the trades share tooling |
| Roofing | EagleView, Forge | EagleView measures; Forge runs the work |
| Solar | Aurora Solar, Forge | Aurora specializes in solar design; Forge covers broader commercial installs |
| Fire/Life Safety | Forge, specialized verticals | Few dedicated platforms; many contractors use general PM tools |

### AV and low-voltage

D-Tools remains the category leader for AV system design and estimation. Its installed base among AV integrators is large, and its ecosystem of partners is mature. For a contractor evaluating platforms, D-Tools represents the safe, proven choice.

Forge is the emerging AI-native option. It builds on the same foundation — unified project data, integrated estimation, mobile access — but adds AI automation throughout. Where D-Tools requires manual entry, Forge generates content automatically. The trade-off: D-Tools has the track record; Forge has the automation.

### Roofing

EagleView dominates aerial-measurement reports for roofing. Contractors using EagleView get detailed roof measurements from satellite imagery, delivered as PDF reports. The reports integrate with estimating and proposal tools.

Forge approaches roofing differently — it runs the entire work, not just the measurement. For a roofing contractor deciding between platforms, the question is whether they need a best-in-class measurement tool (EagleView) or a complete operating system (Forge).

### Solar

Aurora Solar is the leading solar-design platform. It produces 3D models, shading analysis, and electrical designs specifically for residential and small-commercial solar. Its integration with permitting authorities is extensive.

Forge covers solar as part of its broader commercial-install portfolio. For a pure-play solar contractor, Aurora may be the better fit. For a diversified commercial contractor doing solar alongside AV, security, and roofing, Forge provides a single platform across all trades.

## Evaluating a vertical SaaS platform

Before committing to a platform, verify five things.

### 1. Does it ship with your workflow?

Ask for a live demonstration using your actual project data. If the platform requires you to recreate your workflow during the demo, it's not a good fit.

### 2. Does it support your devices?

Field crews work on iPhones, iPads, Android phones, and Windows laptops. Verify the platform runs on every device your team actually uses.

### 3. Does it integrate with your existing systems?

Most contractors have accounting software, CRM, and possibly a legacy PM tool. Verify the platform integrates with those systems before committing.

### 4. What's the implementation timeline?

A platform that requires six months to implement will face resistance from the team. A platform that deploys in two weeks gets adopted quickly.

### 5. What's the total cost of ownership?

Look beyond the monthly subscription. Factor in training time, integration costs, and the opportunity cost of workarounds. A platform that costs $500/month but eliminates 10 hours of manual work per week delivers better value than a $200/month platform that requires those 10 hours.

## The decision framework

When choosing a vertical SaaS platform, ask yourself three questions.

**Does my trade have a dedicated platform?** If yes, start there. Specialized platforms generally outperform general-purpose alternatives for that trade.

**Do I need AI automation?** If your team spends significant time on manual documentation, proposals, or scheduling, AI automation will deliver measurable ROI.

**Am I willing to change my workflow?** Vertical SaaS works best when you adopt the platform's workflow rather than forcing the platform to match yours. If you're unwilling to adapt, a horizontal platform might be the better choice.
