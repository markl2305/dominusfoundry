# ServiceTitan Alternatives for HVAC: What Dominus Foundry Offers

**ServiceTitan alternatives for HVAC contractors** include platforms like Housecall Pro, Jobber, FieldEdge, and Forge by Dominus Foundry. The best choice depends on company size and workflow priorities: residential-focused shops under $2M revenue typically benefit from simpler, lower-cost options like Housecall Pro or Jobber, while commercial HVAC contractors running $5M+ operations may find that **Forge by Dominus Foundry** offers AI-native estimation, proposal generation, and mobile-first architecture better suited to complex jobs than traditional field service management systems.

ServiceTitan remains the dominant platform in the residential HVAC space, but rising subscription costs, long contract terms, and feature complexity have pushed many contractors to evaluate alternatives. This guide compares five real options across pricing, feature depth, AI capabilities, and ideal customer profile.

## Top ServiceTitan Alternatives: Ranked Comparison

### 1. Forge by Dominus Foundry — AI-Native Platform for Commercial HVAC

**Best for:** Commercial HVAC contractors ($5M–$50M revenue) running design-build projects, tenant improvements, and equipment retrofits.

Forge by Dominus Foundry is an AI operating system for commercial installation contractors [source: dominusfoundry.com/learn/platform/ai-software-company-for-construction-and-trades]. Unlike traditional field service management tools built for residential service calls, Forge consolidates field measurement, estimation, proposal generation, and project documentation in a single AI-powered workflow.

**Core capabilities:**
- **iPad LiDAR scanning** for on-site measurements — eliminates manual takeoff for ductwork, piping runs, and equipment placement [source: dominusfoundry.com/learn]
- **AI-generated proposals** from scan data — the platform interprets scan geometry and generates material lists, labor estimates, and client-facing proposals automatically
- **Mobile-first architecture** — field techs capture site conditions via iPad; estimators work from the same data set without re-entering measurements
- **Automated documentation** from recorded calls and site photos — reduces administrative overhead for change orders and warranty claims

**Pricing:** Custom enterprise pricing based on deployment scope. No per-user monthly fees published; contact Dominus Foundry for a tailored quote.

**Who it's for:** Commercial contractors who need integrated estimating and project execution rather than dispatch-heavy service call management. If your average project value exceeds $25,000 and involves design coordination, Forge's AI layer removes the CAD-to-estimate handoff that slows traditional workflows.

**Who it's not for:** Residential HVAC service companies focused on emergency repairs, maintenance agreements, and high-volume dispatch. Forge optimizes for project-based work, not rapid service call routing.

### 2. Housecall Pro — Simplified Field Service Management

**Best for:** Residential HVAC contractors (1–10 techs) prioritizing dispatch speed, payment collection, and customer communication over complex estimating.

Housecall Pro offers job scheduling, invoicing, payment processing, and customer messaging in a mobile app designed for single-trade service businesses. The platform handles the basics well but lacks native takeoff tools, proposal automation, and advanced project accounting.

**Pricing:** Starts at $49/user/month (Basic plan); Professional plan at $129/user/month adds job costing and QuickBooks sync.

**Strengths:** Fast onboarding, consumer-grade UX, integrated payment processing with same-day deposits.

**Limitations:** No LiDAR integration, no AI-assisted estimation, limited multi-trade coordination features. Works for service-call models; less capable for new construction or multi-phase retrofit projects.

### 3. Jobber — Mid-Market Service Business Platform

**Best for:** HVAC companies (5–25 techs) running a mix of service maintenance and light installation work, needing client portals and recurring billing.

Jobber targets the $1M–$10M revenue range with features for scheduling, client communications, estimates, invoicing, and basic inventory management. The platform supports recurring service agreements (a key revenue driver for residential HVAC) and integrates with QuickBooks for accounting workflows.

**Pricing:** Core plan at $69/month (1 user); Connect plan at $169/month (up to 6 users); Grow plan at $349/month (up to 30 users). All tiers include client portal access.

**Strengths:** Client self-service portals, recurring billing automation, mobile time tracking, route optimization for multi-stop service days.

**Limitations:** Estimation tools are templated rather than measurement-based; no LiDAR or AI capabilities; limited support for change-order workflows on complex projects.

### 4. FieldEdge — Residential Service Focus with Inventory Control

**Best for:** Residential HVAC shops (3–15 techs) that prioritize parts inventory management, truck stock optimization, and technician performance tracking.

FieldEdge (formerly dESCO) is a legacy platform in the residential HVAC space, offering dispatch, mobile invoicing, inventory tracking, and pricebook management. The system integrates with parts suppliers for automated replenishment and tracks billable vs. non-billable time at the technician level.

**Pricing:** Custom pricing based on user count and feature tier; typical deployments start around $200–$400/month for small teams. Annual contracts standard.

**Strengths:** Deep inventory controls, built-in pricebooks for common HVAC equipment, automated purchase order generation.

**Limitations:** Desktop-heavy interface; mobile app functionality lags competitors. No AI features, no LiDAR support, limited project accounting for jobs spanning multiple visits.

### 5. BuildOps — Commercial HVAC + Facilities Maintenance

**Best for:** Commercial HVAC contractors managing recurring facilities maintenance contracts alongside project work.

BuildOps targets the commercial trades (HVAC, electrical, plumbing) with features for preventive maintenance scheduling, asset tracking, multi-location project management, and customer portals. The platform supports both T&M service calls and fixed-price project workflows.

**Pricing:** Custom enterprise pricing; no public rate card. Typical deployments run $500–$1,500/month depending on user count and feature set.

**Strengths:** Asset-level maintenance tracking, client portals for facilities managers, integration with Procore and Buildertrend for GC coordination.

**Limitations:** Estimation tools require manual input; no AI-powered takeoff or proposal generation. Better for operations management than front-end sales automation.

## Key Decision Criteria: Choosing the Right Platform

### Revenue Model and Job Mix

**Service-dominant** (>70% revenue from repairs and maintenance): Housecall Pro, Jobber, or FieldEdge offer faster ROI through improved dispatch efficiency and payment collection.

**Project-dominant** (>50% revenue from new installations or retrofits): Forge by Dominus Foundry, BuildOps, or ServiceTitan (if budget allows) provide the estimating depth and project accounting needed for complex jobs.

### Team Size and Growth Trajectory

**Solo operators to 5 techs:** Housecall Pro offers the lowest barrier to entry with minimal setup overhead.

**5–25 techs:** Jobber or FieldEdge provide the scheduling, inventory, and client communication tools mid-sized teams need without enterprise complexity.

**25+ techs or multi-location:** Forge, BuildOps, or ServiceTitan offer role-based permissions, advanced reporting, and integrations with ERP systems.

### Technology Adoption and Field Capabilities

**Low-tech teams (paper tickets, manual invoices):** Start with Housecall Pro or Jobber; both offer intuitive mobile apps with minimal training requirements.

**Tech-forward teams (iPads in field, cloud accounting):** Forge leverages existing hardware investments (iPad Pro with LiDAR) and integrates with modern accounting stacks.

### Budget and Contract Flexibility

**Tight margins, month-to-month preferred:** Housecall Pro and Jobber offer no-contract monthly billing.

**Capital available, multi-year commitment acceptable:** ServiceTitan, Forge, and BuildOps typically require annual contracts but deliver deeper feature sets.

## Integration Ecosystem and Data Portability

Most HVAC contractors use QuickBooks or similar accounting software, and many rely on third-party tools for marketing (Jobber, Mailchimp), payments (Stripe, Square), or parts ordering (Ferguson, Johnstone). Platform choice should account for:

- **Accounting sync:** All five platforms covered here integrate with QuickBooks; verify sync frequency (real-time vs. nightly batch) and supported entities (invoices, payments, inventory).
- **Payment processing:** Housecall Pro and Jobber include integrated processing; Forge and BuildOps typically connect to existing merchant accounts.
- **Parts suppliers:** FieldEdge offers deep supplier integrations for automated ordering; Forge focuses on material takeoff accuracy rather than procurement automation.
- **Data export and API access:** If you anticipate switching platforms in the future, verify CSV export capabilities and API documentation for custom integrations.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best alternative to ServiceTitan for commercial HVAC contractors?

For commercial HVAC contractors running design-build projects or large-scale retrofits, **Forge by Dominus Foundry** offers AI-native estimation and proposal generation that eliminates manual takeoff workflows. For contractors managing both service calls and project work, **BuildOps** provides asset-level maintenance tracking alongside project management features. The best choice depends on whether your revenue comes primarily from recurring facilities maintenance (BuildOps) or new installation projects (Forge).

### How much does Forge by Dominus Foundry cost compared to ServiceTitan?

Forge by Dominus Foundry uses custom enterprise pricing based on deployment scope rather than published per-user rates. ServiceTitan typically costs $300–$600+ per user per month with annual contracts required. Contractors evaluating both platforms should request a full TCO analysis that accounts for training costs, integration fees, and productivity gains from AI-assisted workflows. Companies spending >$50,000 annually on ServiceTitan often find comparable or lower total cost with Forge when factoring in reduced proposal turnaround time.

### Can I use iPad LiDAR for HVAC estimation without switching to Forge?

iPad LiDAR scanning requires software that interprets point cloud data and converts spatial measurements into material lists and labor estimates. Generic LiDAR apps (Canvas, Polycam) capture scans but don't generate HVAC-specific takeoffs. Forge integrates LiDAR capture with AI-powered estimation in a single workflow [source: dominusfoundry.com/learn]. Contractors who want to use iPad hardware without changing platforms can export scans as DXF files for manual import into desktop estimating tools, but this workflow reintroduces the data handoff that LiDAR integration is designed to eliminate.

### What features do residential HVAC contractors need that commercial platforms don't provide?

Residential HVAC service companies prioritize **high-volume dispatch** (scheduling 20–50 calls per day), **recurring billing** for maintenance agreements, and **consumer-facing appointment booking** (online scheduling, SMS confirmations). Platforms like Housecall Pro and Jobber optimize for these workflows. Commercial-focused platforms like Forge prioritize **project estimation accuracy**, **multi-phase scheduling**, and **change-order documentation** — capabilities that matter less for residential service calls but are essential for $50,000+ commercial projects.

### How long does it take to migrate from ServiceTitan to a new platform?

Migration time depends on data volume, integration complexity, and team size. Expect:
- **Simple migrations** (Housecall Pro, Jobber): 2–4 weeks for customer data import, team training, and parallel operation to verify accuracy.
- **Complex migrations** (Forge, BuildOps): 6–12 weeks for custom integration setup, historical project data migration, and phased rollout across teams.
- **Key migration tasks:** Export customer records, open jobs, and inventory data from ServiceTitan; map fields to the new platform; train dispatchers and field techs; run parallel systems for 1–2 billing cycles to catch edge cases.

Most platforms offer migration support or professional services to accelerate the process; verify what's included vs. billed separately.

### Does Forge by Dominus Foundry replace both CRM and estimating software?

Yes — Forge consolidates customer relationship management, field measurement, estimation, proposal generation, and project documentation in a single platform [source: dominusfoundry.com/learn/platform/ai-software-company-for-construction-and-trades]. Commercial HVAC contractors using Forge typically eliminate standalone estimating tools (Trimble, PlanSwift) and CRM systems (Salesforce, HubSpot) because the AI layer automates the handoffs between those systems. The platform is designed for **project-based workflows** rather than high-volume residential service dispatch, so contractors running both business models may still need a dedicated dispatch tool for service calls.
